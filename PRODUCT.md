# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Enterprise software buyers, CIOs, and senior practitioners who encounter Sachin through cold outreach, LinkedIn, or referral and want to understand who he is before a meeting. AI/tech builders curious about someone publicly working on agents, trading systems, and LLM pipelines. Potential investors, co-founders, or collaborators evaluating his thinking. The primary job: arrive skeptical, leave convinced this person operates at a different level.

## Product Purpose

sachinai.com is Sachin Rai's personal brand site — a blog and portfolio that proves he builds, not just sells. Every post is a proof point: real systems shipped (AutoQuant, ARIA, SwingTrader), real capital at risk, real sales closed. The site succeeds when a visitor comes away thinking "this person is an operator, not a talker." It is also the prototype — if Sachin is selling AI to enterprises, the site should itself be evidence of what he can build.

## Brand Personality

**Precise. Premium. Builder.**

Voice: direct, data-backed, no hedging. Shows work rather than claims. "I found that" not "I feel like." Numbers, systems, outcomes.

Tone: confident but quiet. Never performs importance. The work speaks.

Emotional goal: the reader should feel a slight envy — like they're looking at someone a few moves ahead.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS blog** — Tailwind card grids, Inter everywhere, gradient blobs, teal CTAs, stock photos of laptops. The visual shorthand for "I used a template."
- **Hustle-bro newsletter** — orange urgency CTAs, "I made $X in 30 days" energy, aggressive upsell above the fold, fake scarcity.
- **Cluttered consultant site** — testimonials wall, logo parade, 15 competing sections all demanding attention. Trying to prove credibility through volume instead of substance.

## Design Principles

1. **The prototype is the close.** The site itself is evidence. Every design decision should demonstrate the same standard Sachin applies to his actual work — precise, intentional, no sloppy defaults.
2. **Signal without shouting.** Premium is expressed through restraint, craft, and confidence. Never through loudness, motion for motion's sake, or decorative complexity.
3. **One job per section.** Each section has one purpose. No competing CTAs, no hedging layouts, no "and also..." moments.
4. **Earn every element.** If something doesn't serve the content or the reader, it shouldn't be there. Whitespace is structural, not filler.
5. **Show the work.** Posts reference real systems with real outputs. The design should frame this as signal — not blog posts, field notes from an operator.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected (implemented). Strong ink-on-white contrast throughout. No color-only information encoding.
